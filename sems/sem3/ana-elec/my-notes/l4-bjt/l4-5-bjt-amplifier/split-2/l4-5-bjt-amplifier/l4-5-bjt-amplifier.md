### Amplifier Configurations

- Three amplifier configurations are:
- 1. Common-emitter.
- 2. Common-base.
- 3. Common-collector.

{8}------------------------------------------------

#### 1. Common-Base

An amplifier that uses an emitter input signal to produce a collector output signal.

The most widely used type of BJT amplifier; it has the emitter as the reference

#### 2. Common-Emitter.

#### Example:

a. Determine βac and βdc from the collector characteristics

$$\beta_{\text{ac}} = \frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta I_B} \Big|_{V_{\text{CE-constant}}} = \frac{I_{C_2} - I_{C_1}}{I_{B_2} - I_{B_1}}$$

$$= \frac{3.2 \text{ mA} - 2.2 \text{ mA}}{30 \mu \text{A} - 20 \mu \text{A}} = \frac{1 \text{ mA}}{10 \mu \text{A}}$$

$$= 100$$

$$\beta_{\text{dc}} = \frac{I_C}{I_B} = \frac{2.7 \text{ mA}}{25 \mu \text{A}} = 10$$
